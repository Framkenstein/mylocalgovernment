# My Local Government.com

Making democracy readable, one city at a time.

## 🎯 Mission

There is a fundamental paradox in American politics: the level of government that impacts our daily lives the most is the one we pay the least attention to. While national elections dominate the news, it is your local city council that decides whether an affordable housing project gets built, how much funding goes to public safety, whether your street gets a bike lane, and how high your local taxes will be.

Our philosophy is simple: **Data Over Rhetoric**. We build tools to strip away the administrative burden of local civics, allowing residents to judge their representatives on their actions, not their campaign slogans.

## 🌟 Features

### Council Member Scorecards
- **Voting Analysis**: Track council votes across 5 key policy areas
- **Alignment Scores**: Clear percentages showing policy alignment
- **Detailed Breakdowns**: Individual vote records with methodology
- **Color-Coded Performance**: Visual indicators at a glance

### Policy Categories
1. **Housing Development** - Support for new housing, zoning reforms, and affordability
2. **Public Safety** - Police, fire, emergency services funding
3. **Economic Development** - Business growth, job creation, economic vitality
4. **Transportation** - Infrastructure, bike lanes, public transit, mobility
5. **Environment** - Climate action, conservation, sustainability initiatives

### Budget Portal
- **Fiscal Transparency**: Comprehensive budget breakdowns in layperson terms
- **Interactive Charts**: Visual representation of revenue and spending
- **Official Sources**: Direct links to City of Santa Cruz budget documents
- **Tax Reality Check**: Understanding where your tax dollars go

### Scoring Methodology
```
Score = Pro Votes / Total Votes Present
```
- **Fair**: Only votes where members were present count
- **Transparent**: Clear methodology for each category
- **Objective**: Based on actual voting records, not rhetoric

## 🏙️ Santa Cruz Pilot

We're launching in Santa Cruz, California - the perfect proving ground:
- **Total Budget**: $533.8 million for Fiscal Year 2026
- **General Fund**: $158.9 million (completely balanced)
- **Complex Challenges**: Housing crisis, environmental sustainability
- **Engaged Community**: Ready for data-driven civic engagement

### Current Coverage
- **161 Council Decisions** analyzed (Jan 2025 - Jan 2026)
- **7 Council Members** tracked across all categories
- **100% Environment Consensus** - Unanimous support for environmental initiatives
- **Comprehensive Budget Data** - FY 2026 financial breakdown with official sources
